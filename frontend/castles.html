Introduction

Castles is a turn based card game meant to be played with themed poker cards, but plain old Bicycle cards will work just as well. The game can be played with just the rules on this page, or each player can use the special rules from any one of the decks on the Decks page

Setting Up

• Each player shuffles their deck: a standard 52-card deck with 2 jokers added.

• The Wall: Each player sets 5 cards on the table face-down in landscape orientation.

• The Garrison: Each player sets 3 cards on the table face-down in portrait orientation.

• The Hand: Each player draws 3 cards from the deck as their starting hand. Players may keep their hand hidden from other players.

• Randomly determine a player to take the first turn, then players take turns proceeding clockwise.

Taking a Turn

• Draw until you have 5 cards in your hand.

• The player to your left at the start of your turn is your opponent for this turn.

• Play one card as your army, and one card as your general for this turn.

• With your army card, you must attack your opponent's wall cards until your army card is defeated or your opponent has no cards remaining on their wall.

• To attack: Select any of your opponent's wall cards. If that card is face-down, flip it face-up. Compare the number on the two cards, the card with the lower number is usually defeated and placed into the discard pile, with some exceptions:

◦ Aces have a value of 1. Jacks are 11, Queens are 12, and Kings are 13.

◦ If the lower number is exactly half the value of the higher number, the lower number wins. (Note that this means Sixes beat Queens)

◦ Aces defeat any face cards.

◦ Aces defeat any card that shares a suit with them.

◦ A joker cannot attack; when a joker would attack it uses its ability instead. To use the joker, you have three options:

1. Cannon: Remove a card on any player's wall.

2. Reveal: Reveal all cards on any player's wall.

3. Reinforce: Add two cards to any player's wall.

◦ Any card that attacks a Joker is defeated, and the joker also uses its ability.

◦ When attacking as a general, the Joker defeats any card.

• If the two cards have the same number, both are defeated.

• If your army is defeated, the wall card remains face-up on the wall.

• If your army is NOT defeated, you must attack again until the army is defeated or your opponent's wall is empty.

• If your opponent's wall is empty, you then attack their garrison cards with your general until your general is defeated or your opponent has no cards remaining in their garrison.

◦ If you fail to defeat all of the cards in your opponent's garrison, your opponent immediately rebuilds their wall with 5 new cards from the top of their deck.

◦ If you defeat all of the cards in your opponent's garrison, that player loses and your turn is over.

Winning

• When a player has no cards remaining in their garrison, that player loses and takes no more turns.

• The last player with any cards in their garrison wins.

Other Rules

• When a player has no cards remaining in their deck, that player shuffles their discard pile back into their deck.

• When a joker is revealed anywhere other than being played as a general, it uses its ability immediately and is then discarded.

• If any player's last wall card is removed by means other than an attacking army (such as a Joker's Cannon ability), the player to their right plays a general from their hand to attack the garrison.

◦ This does not change the turn order.

• If a player must play a card from their hand but their hand is empty, they play the top card of their deck instead.

Keywords

• Play: Use this ability when you play this card as either your army or your general.

• Army: Use this ability when you play this card as your army.

• General: Use this ability when you play this card as your general.

• Reaction: Use this ability during any player's turn by discarding this card from your hand. There will sometimes be a triggering event that must occur before you can play this.

Decks

The Stormlight Archive

Stormlight Archive Abilities

K The Blackthorn

Play: You and a player of your choice each discard a card from your garrison.

Q Artifabrian

Play: Add a card from your hand to your wall face-down.

J Legendary Duelist

Army: The first time this card would be defeated, it wins instead.

K Windrunner

General: Place a card from your hand face-up onto your wall.

Q Honorspren

Play: Select a face-up numbered card on your wall and turn it 45 degrees. While it remains on your wall, it is now a King.

J Bridge Four Stew

Army: Play another army card. You can use either one each time you attack this turn, until either one is defeated.

K Truthless

General: This card defeats aces and jokers this turn. When defeated, the joker's ability does not activate. Your opponent chooses which cards you attack with your army and general this turn.

Q Lift & Wyndle

Play: Draw three cards, then discard a card.

J Nightblood

Army: You must discard a card on any player's wall, then that player may discard a card on your wall. If that player chooses to do so, repeat this effect.

K Soulcaster

Reaction: Swap your opponent's army and general.

Q Lightweaving

Play: Pick up any player's wall, shuffle it, and place it back face-down.

J Warform

Reaction: When a card is attacked, increase its number value by 3 during that attack.

Court of the Dead Abilities

K Grave Lord

Play: Move up to three numbered cards or aces from your discard pile to your wall.

Q Fallen Paladin

Play: Move an ace from your discard pile to your garrison face-up.

J Skeletal Chaplain

Play: Move a numbered card from your discard pile to your garrison face-up.

K Bloodshaping

Reaction: Even numbers defeat odd numbers this turn.

Q The Sacrifice

Reaction: Discard a card from your wall. No other cards can be removed from your wall this turn.

J Night Stalker

Army: Discard a face-down card from all other player's walls.

K Soulbinder

Play: Choose a card on your wall and turn it 45 degrees. As long as that card remains on your wall, in a turn where that card is defeated and your entire wall is not defeated, return it to your wall at the end of the turn.

Q Demon Caller

General: For each face-up card on your opponent's wall, play the top card of your deck to fight it.

J Abomination

Army: Combine two even or two odd numbered cards from your discard into one card with the sum of their values and place it onto your wall face-up.

K Forbidden Tome

General: Choose a player, each of you discards a card from your hand. The player whose card defeats the other's moves a face card from their discard onto their wall face-up.

Q Silent Occultist

Reaction: Another card's ability is ignored this turn.

J Chained Reaper

Army: Cards defeated from an opponent's wall are added to your wall. Keep them separate, when discarded they return to their owner's discard pile.
